Heat Island Score

Measure urban heat exposure using 5-year climate data, building density, and green space coverage. Higher score means cooler location.

What It Measures

The Heat Island Score estimates how hot a location gets during summer relative to surrounding areas. Urban heat islands form where concrete and asphalt absorb and re-radiate heat, while parks and vegetation provide cooling. A higher score means a cooler, more comfortable location.

Calculation Steps

  1. Summer temperature — Fetch 5-year historical daily max temperatures for summer months (Dec-Feb Southern Hemisphere). Compute mean and P90 (90th percentile).
  2. Effective temperature — Blend: effective = mean * 0.4 + P90 * 0.6 (extreme days weighted more).
  3. Temperature score — Normalize against reference range: 22 C (cool) to 35 C (extreme).
  4. Building density penalty — Count buildings within 500m. Dense areas (500+ buildings) lose up to 15 points.
  5. Green space bonus — Count parks/gardens within 1km. Areas with 20+ green spaces gain up to 5 points.

Formula

score = temp_score - density_penalty + green_bonus

Where temp_score = (35 - effective_temp) / (35 - 22) * 100

Limitations

  • Temperature grid is ~10km — cannot capture block-level variation
  • Building density requires local Overture data (currently Melbourne only)
  • No impervious surface data (asphalt coverage is a better UHI proxy)
  • No wind corridor or sea breeze modeling
