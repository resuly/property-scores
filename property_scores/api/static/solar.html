What It Measures

The Solar Potential Score rates a location's suitability for rooftop solar panels on a scale of 0 to 100. It combines satellite-derived solar irradiance data with building geometry to estimate how much energy solar panels could generate at a specific address.

0-20Poor
21-40Low

61-80Good

Calculation Steps

  1. Solar irradiance lookup — Fetch annual GHI (Global Horizontal Irradiance) and PVOUT (Photovoltaic Output potential, kWh/kWp/year) from Global Solar Atlas for the target coordinate.
  2. Score normalization — Map PVOUT to 0-100 against the global range (600 kWh/kWp in Nordic regions to 2400 kWh/kWp in the Sahara).
  3. Orientation adjustment — Apply a factor based on roof orientation: optimal-facing = 1.0, east/west = 0.85, suboptimal = 0.65.
  4. Annual generation estimate — If roof area is provided: kWh = roof_m2 x 0.20 x PVOUT x orientation x 0.80

Formula

score = (PVOUT - 600) / (2400 - 600) x 100 x orientation_factor

Clamped to 0-100. A score of 50 means the location receives roughly the global median solar irradiance.

What This Score Does NOT Include

  • Actual roof pitch angle (assumes near-optimal tilt)
  • Roof obstructions (chimneys, vents, HVAC units)
  • Tree shading (would require LiDAR or high-res imagery)
  • Local grid feed-in tariff or electricity pricing
  • Panel degradation over time
